DJP: Database for JAMAC Problems

The DJP is where all present, past (until 2024), and future problems will be stored for usage and inspiration for future competitions.

How do I get access to the problems?

Anyone can make an account to access the DJP, however, only Albemarle High School's Math Honor Society and Math Club may access unused problems. There are three tiers of accounts in the DJP: Noob, Pro, and Hacker. Noob-level accounts can only access previously used problems. Pro-level accounts, reserved for MHS, can access and create new problems for the next competitions. Hacker-level accounts, reserved for the Math Club, can create, modify, delete, and mark problems as used.

Once an account is created, a new tab will appear at the top of the page, allowing you to access the database.

How do I promote my account?

The general public is only allowed to use the lowest tier of account. If you are a MHS or Math Club member, email Mr. Woodward and have him promote your account.
